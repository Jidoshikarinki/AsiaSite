Vietnam War

The Vietnam War, lasting from 1955 to 1975, was a conflict between the communist forces of North Vietnam and the government of South Vietnam, supported by the United States and other anti-communist allies. It was a result of the Cold War rivalry and the desire of North Vietnam to unify the country under communist rule. The war was marked by guerrilla warfare, heavy casualties, and significant political turmoil. It ended with the fall of Saigon in 1975 and the reunification of Vietnam under communist control. The war had a profound impact on Vietnam and the world, shaping geopolitics and influencing military strategies for years to come.

Korean War

The Korean War, from 1950 to 1953, was a conflict between North Korea, supported by China and the Soviet Union, and South Korea, backed by the United Nations, particularly the United States. It began with North Korea's invasion of South Korea to unify the country under communism. The UN, led by the U.S., intervened to support South Korea, leading to intense fighting and many casualties. The war ended in a stalemate, with the Korean Peninsula divided along the 38th parallel, similar to before the war. It had lasting effects, solidifying the division between North and South Korea, shaping U.S. foreign policy in Asia, and impacting Cold War dynamics.

Russia-Ukraine War

The Ukraine-Russia war began in 2014 after Russia annexed Crimea and supported separatists in eastern Ukraine. The conflict has led to thousands of deaths and displaced many. Efforts to resolve it through ceasefire agreements and peace talks continue, but the situation remains volatile. The war has strained relations between Russia and the West and raised questions about sovereignty and territorial integrity.

Indo-Pakistani War

The India-Pakistan wars refer to conflicts over the Kashmir region since 1947. They include the wars of 1947-48, 1965, 1971, and the Kargil War of 1999. These wars have resulted in significant loss of life and have had lasting political and social impacts. The Kashmir issue remains unresolved, leading to ongoing tensions between the two countries.

Israel Gaza War

The Israel-Gaza conflict is a protracted dispute over land, resources, and political control, with roots dating back to the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It revolves around the competing national aspirations of Israelis and Palestinians. The conflict has resulted in numerous casualties and widespread destruction, with efforts to resolve it diplomatically largely unsuccessful. Key issues include the status of Jerusalem, claimed by both sides as their capital, and the Israeli occupation of Palestinian territories, which Palestinians view as a violation of their rights. Despite international calls for peace, achieving a lasting resolution remains challenging due to deep-seated mistrust and complex geopolitical factors.
